Motor Insurance Survey Application (Offline & Online) — Case Study

Project Overview

The Motor Insurance Survey Application is a Flutter-based mobile system developed exclusively for motor insurance surveyors. The application enables field surveyors to conduct vehicle inspections, document damages, and prepare motor survey data in both offline and online environments, with seamless synchronization to the central system.

Business Context

Motor insurance surveys are often conducted at accident sites, workshops, or remote locations where internet access is unreliable. Survey data must be captured accurately in the field and delivered to the insurance company without delays or data loss.

Problem

The traditional motor survey process suffered from operational and data integrity issues:

  • Paper-based or inconsistent digital inspection formats
  • Loss of survey data due to unstable connectivity
  • Delayed submission of motor survey reports
  • Manual re-entry of inspection data into central systems
  • Lack of standardization across surveyors

Solution

I developed an offline-first Flutter application that allows motor surveyors to capture complete vehicle inspection data without internet access. The application securely synchronizes survey data and images once connectivity is restored, enabling faster processing of motor insurance claims and reports.

Core Features

  • Offline Motor Survey – Capture vehicle details, accident information, damage assessment, parts condition, mileage, and remarks without network dependency.
  • Damage Documentation – Structured damage entry with photographic evidence linked to specific vehicle sections.
  • Local Data Storage – All survey data and images are stored locally to prevent data loss during field operations.
  • Online Synchronization – Background synchronization uploads pending surveys when internet becomes available.
  • Motor Survey Report Generation – Submitted data feeds directly into the central system for motor survey report preparation.

Offline-First Architecture

  • Local database for survey data persistence
  • Queue-based sync mechanism for pending surveys
  • Retry logic for failed uploads
  • Safeguards against duplicate submissions

User Roles

  • Motor Surveyor – Conducts vehicle inspections and submits surveys
  • Reviewer – Reviews survey data and prepares motor insurance reports
  • Admin – Manages users, survey formats, and system configurations

Tech Stack

  • Flutter (Cross-platform mobile application)
  • Laravel (Backend APIs & Integration)
  • MySQL (Centralized data storage)
  • Local Offline Storage (Mobile)

My Responsibilities

  • Flutter application development and architecture
  • Offline data handling and synchronization logic
  • Damage assessment workflows
  • Backend API integration
  • End-to-end motor survey workflow implementation

Impact

  • Reliable motor surveys without internet dependency
  • Faster submission and processing of survey reports
  • Reduced errors through standardized inspection formats
  • Improved coordination between field surveyors and office teams
  • Better auditability and data accuracy

Conclusion

The Motor Insurance Survey Application streamlined field inspections and reporting for motor insurance claims by enabling offline data capture, reliable synchronization, and consistent survey documentation across all surveyors.
